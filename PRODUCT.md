# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Bank executives and innovation leaders (CTO, CDO, Head of Digital) watching a live demo of ElevenLabs Conversational AI applied to financial services. They are evaluating whether to invest in the technology for their institution. They sit across the table from an ElevenLabs salesperson; the Cockpit is on the screen. Their job-to-be-done: quickly assess whether this AI can replace or augment their human advisors. Trust, competence, and technical credibility must be earned in under 5 seconds.

## Product Purpose

FSI Cockpit is an ElevenLabs demo product for financial services. It shows an investment advisor's workspace — client portfolio, AI-analyzed conversations, proactive recommendations — powered by ElevenLabs Conversational AI. Success means a decision-maker sees the demo and immediately understands the value: "our advisors could use this." It is not a production SaaS; it is a persuasion artifact that must look production-ready.

## Brand Personality

ElevenLabs-native. Tech-forward. Authoritative without arrogance.

The surface should feel like something ElevenLabs built for itself, then adapted for financial services — not like a generic fintech product with an ElevenLabs logo bolted on. Dark mode is identity, not aesthetic. The AI is the product; everything else is context that gives the AI room to be remarkable.

Three words: **Precise. Alive. Yours.**

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS (Notion/Linear-clone)**: beige backgrounds, light-mode eyebrow labels on every section, pastel icon backgrounds, identical card grids, JetBrains Mono used as decoration. The current design leans too close to this family.
- **BI dashboard (Tableau/PowerBI)**: chart-heavy, colorful, feels like a quarterly report rather than a relationship tool.
- **Corporate CRM (Salesforce/HubSpot)**: heavy nav, blue corporate, enterprise-of-2012 visual language, modal-first.
- **Consumer fintech (Nubank/Inter)**: saturated purples, gradients, illustration, B2C emotional tone — wrong register for a B2B demo to bank executives.

## Design Principles

1. **The AI is the hero.** ElevenLabs features (real-time voice, transcript analysis, suggested messages, sentiment) must be the most visually prominent elements on every screen. AI-generated content gets a distinct visual language; human data is context.

2. **Dark by conviction.** ElevenLabs ships dark. This product is ElevenLabs. Light surfaces are used sparingly for content emphasis (cards inside panels), never as the primary ground.

3. **Decisor-confidence in 5 seconds.** The decision-maker must read competence and precision from the first glance. Dense information presented with clear hierarchy, never clutter. Every number, label, and status must be immediately meaningful.

4. **Show don't label.** The interface demonstrates AI capability through behavior and content, not through badges saying "AI-powered." An ElevenAgent analysis card that simply radiates quality speaks louder than a "✨ AI" eyebrow.

5. **Rhythm earns density.** Financial data is dense by nature. The design earns this density through consistent spacing rhythm, restrained color, and clear typographic hierarchy — not by reducing information.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1 body text, 3:1 large text) — critical on dark backgrounds where contrast errors are common.
- Reduced motion must be respected; the AI indicators (EQ bars, pulse animations) have static fallbacks.
- Color must never be the sole carrier of status information (use shape + color, not color alone).
